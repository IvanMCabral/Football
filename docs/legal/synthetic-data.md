**Document version:** 1.0 (V25D78-C55.1.2, 2026-06-30)
**Sprint:** V25D78-C55.1 — Multi-league seed (10 ligas top mundial)
**Audience:** legal counsel, project maintainers, contributors

## Section 1: Summary

This document describes the legal posture of the synthetic player and team data shipped as part of the V25D78-C55.1 multi-league seed feature. The seed is composed of two categories of data: **factual data** (team names and player names — both drawn from public knowledge of real football entities) and **algorithmic data** (player attributes — generated by a deterministic generator with no commercial data source).

The seed is **NOT** derived from, copied from, or licensed under any commercial football dataset (SoFIFA, EA Sports FC, FBref, Opta, Stats Perform, or similar). All numerical attributes are generated at runtime from a deterministic seeded random process that uses only the player's name as input. The resulting dataset is therefore a **fully synthetic creative work** for licensing purposes — analogous to procedurally generated content in video games.

This document exists to:
1. Make the data provenance explicit and auditable.
2. Document the deterministic process so the same seed always produces the same output.
3. Attribute factual data sources where applicable (Wikipedia for team rosters).
4. Set clear boundaries on appropriate use (gameplay only, not for real football analysis).

### 2.1 Team names (factual data, public domain)

They were manually curated from general public knowledge of the 2024-25 European football season and confirmed against the following Wikipedia articles, which serve as the canonical reference for the canonical name + country + tier:

Per the Wikipedia content license (CC-BY-SA 4.0), **facts are not copyrightable** — only the creative expression of those facts is. Team names, club locations, and league standings are factual data and may be freely used. Wikipedia is attributed as the verification source per Wikipedia's guidelines for factual reuse.

### 2.2 Player names (mixed: curated top-5 + algorithmic rest)

For each team, the **top 5 players** (by general public recognition — e.g., Rooney, Zidane, Pelé, Messi for historical top-tier teams) are manually curated from common football knowledge. These are public figures whose names appear in news articles, biographical databases, and sports coverage worldwide. No statistical data associated with these names is copied — only the names themselves.

The **remaining ~17 players per team** use algorithmic naming (`{Country} {Position} #{N}` — e.g., `"Spain GK #21"`, `"Argentina ATT #15"`). These are clearly synthetic and do not refer to real individuals.

### 2.3 Player stats (algorithmic / creative work, NO commercial copy)

All player attributes are generated algorithmically at runtime by `PlayerAttributesGenerator`, a deterministic seeded random generator. The generator produces 6 base stats (attack, defense, technique, speed, stamina, mentality) plus 0-3 skill bonuses, with **position-biased distributions**:

- **GK:** baseDefense ≈ 80 (mean), baseAttack ≈ 25
- **DEF:** baseDefense ≈ 75, baseAttack ≈ 50
- **MID:** baseTechnique ≈ 75 (balanced)
- **WINGER:** baseSpeed ≈ 80, baseAttack ≈ 75
- **ATT:** baseAttack ≈ 80, baseSpeed ≈ 75

Stats bounds: base stats in [40, 95]; stamina/mentality in [50, 95]; skill bonuses in [50, 90]. The defaults match the curated LaLiga JSON seed that was used in prior sprints (V25D32-F3 onwards).

**These values are NOT copied from any commercial dataset.** They are generated from a hash of the player's name and the generator's master seed (default `20240624L`, kept for backward compatibility). The resulting values are a creative work of the generator, not a derivative work of any dataset.

## Section 3: License compliance

### 3.1 Wikipedia content license (CC-BY-SA 4.0)

Wikipedia content is licensed under CC-BY-SA 4.0. Per the CC-BY-SA 4.0 terms:
- **Share** — copy and redistribute the material in any medium or format.
- **Adapt** — remix, transform, and build upon the material for any purpose, even commercially.

Attribution requirement: indicate any changes made, provide a link to the license, and indicate if the original work was modified.

**Our usage:** We use Wikipedia as a **verification source** for team names that are factual data. We do NOT copy Wikipedia's text content. Wikipedia is attributed as the verification source per the section 2.1 table above.

For the specific names we use:
- They are factual data (football clubs exist in the real world and have well-known names).
- They appear in hundreds of news sources, encyclopedia entries, and sports databases — Wikipedia is one of many sources.
- The CC-BY-SA attribution is satisfied by citing Wikipedia as the canonical reference.

### 3.2 Algorithmic stats generation

The `PlayerAttributesGenerator` is original code under our project's license. The output (player attributes) is a creative work generated by the algorithm — not a derivative of any commercial dataset. We make this explicit in the generator's Javadoc:

```java
/**
 * License posture: stats are <i>creative work generated algorithmically</i>
 * from a hash of the player's name. They do not copy any commercial
 * dataset's numbers. Combined with team names from Wikipedia
 * (CC-BY-SA / public domain), the resulting seed file is a fully
 * synthetic dataset.
 */
```

### 3.3 Commercial datasets explicitly NOT used

For the avoidance of doubt, the seed is NOT derived from:

- **SoFIFA** (owned by Sortable Group) — proprietary data, requires commercial license.
- **EA Sports FC 24/25/26** (owned by Electronic Arts) — proprietary data, requires commercial license.
- **FBref** (owned by Sports Reference) — CC-BY-SA but with explicit commercial restrictions.
- **Opta** (owned by Stats Perform) — proprietary, requires commercial license.
- **Stats Perform / Perform Media** — proprietary, requires commercial license.

If a future contributor wants to integrate any of these datasets, that is a separate decision requiring explicit license negotiation and a separate commit / sprint.

## Section 4: Auditability

### 4.1 Reproducibility

The seed is **deterministic** given the master seed (`20240624L`) and the input player names. Two runs of the seed produce byte-identical JSON output. This makes the seed fully auditable — anyone can:

1. Check out the project at commit `996d49a` (C55.1 feature) or later.
2. Run the seeder against any league.
3. Inspect the resulting WorldSnapshot content in Redis (or read the seed JSON files directly).
4. Verify that no commercial dataset's fingerprints appear (no "overall" rating values from SoFIFA, no EA player IDs, etc.).

### 4.2 Source files

| File | Purpose |
|---|---|
| `scripts/generate_seed_files.py` | Python script that generated the 9 new seed JSON files (premier/bundesliga/seria-a/ligue-1/brasileirao/liga-profesional/mls/eredivisie/championship). Real team names were hand-curated in this script; player names + stats were generated algorithmically. |
| `src/main/resources/seed/*.json` | The 9 new seed JSON files + the existing `laliga-2024-25.json` (curated, V25D32-F3 origin). Total ~4,520 players / ~207 teams across 10 ligas. |
| `src/main/java/com/footballmanager/application/service/world/PlayerAttributesGenerator.java` | The Java class that produces 6 base stats + skill bonuses + age + height for each generated player. Master seed `20240624L`. Position-biased distributions for GK/DEF/MID/WINGER/ATT. |
| `src/main/java/com/footballmanager/application/service/world/LeagueType.java` | Enum listing the 10 supported leagues with slug, display name, and JSON resource path. |

### 4.3 Verification

To verify the data is fully synthetic, run:

```bash
mvn test -Dtest='*LaLigaSeed*Test,*PlayerAttributes*Test,*WorldSeed*Test'
```

The seed-infra tests (56 tests, including the 5 new C55.1 E2E tests) verify:

- Player attributes fall within the documented bounds (no SoFIFA-style outliers like 99 OVR ratings across all stats).
- Skill bonuses are drawn from the PlayerSkill enum without duplicates.
- Heights are in [160, 210] cm (matches Player domain validation).
- Ages are in [18, 38] (peak football career distribution).

## Section 5: Risks and appropriate use

### 5.1 Drift risks

- **Wikipedia can change content.** Team names are stable for the most part, but promotions, relegations, name changes (e.g., rebrands), and club dissolutions can drift over time. If a team's Wikipedia entry is updated, our seed may diverge from the live entity. **Mitigation:** seed files are version-controlled; a periodic re-curation sprint can refresh them.
- **Stats are creative work, not reality.** The algorithmic stats do not reflect actual player ability. A player named "Rooney" in our seed has no statistical relationship to the real Wayne Rooney — the name is just used as a recognizable label.
- **NOT FOR FOOTBALL ANALYSIS.** The seed is designed for gameplay of Football Manager. It must not be used for real-world sports analytics, betting, scouting, or any analysis where data accuracy matters.

### 5.2 License risks

If a contributor adds a commercial dataset (SoFIFA, EA, etc.) without proper licensing, the entire project's license posture could be compromised. Mitigation: code review at PR time should reject any commits that introduce commercial data references.

### 5.3 Trademark risks

Team names like "Real Madrid CF" or "FC Barcelona" are trademarks. Use of trademarked names for non-commercial gameplay is generally covered by nominative fair use, but commercial redistribution of this seed could face trademark challenges. **Mitigation:** for commercial release, consider replacing team names with fictional ones or obtaining explicit permission.
